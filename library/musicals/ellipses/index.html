Plot

Ellipses is a universal musical that stars the Galaxy family and their dog Gravity as they figure their way out of the Big Bang.

Before the world we have come to know, there once lived Singularity, their children, and their unruly dog named Gravity. Their universe turns upside down when their firstborn HD strikes their parent. HD escapes with Gravity to explore The Edge of the Universe. The Big Bang incapacitates Singularity, unleashes Gravity, and dooms everyone else. To restore order, the living family members organize a search party to locate their oldest sibling, HD. They encounter beasts, fight famine, and settle sibling rivalries on this cosmic journey. In the chaos, their baby sibling Milky Way gets separated. Alone, Milky Way must confront the Universe‚Äôs most powerful foe: puberty.

Adapting the origin of the cosmos into an epic family drama, astronomer-songwriter David Quang Pham celebrates family and science with this universal musical, Ellipses. Our Universe is a coming-of-age story that answers the 13.8-billion-year-old question: What is everything?

History

Ellipses had four Working Title Playwrights table readings (October 2020, December 2020, February 2021, and May 2021) with a public concert reading in July 2021. It had concert readings at Bloomfield produced by Colorado New Musicals Festival in July 2022 and an industry workshop production in Theatre 71 at Blessed Sacrament in January 2023. It went international at the Theatre About Science International Conference held in the University of Coimbra in Portugal.

Author

David Quang Pham is an award-winning Vietnamese-American songwriter and filmmaker based in Manhattan, known for creating musicals that personify scientific concepts to explore the human condition. His work has been produced or developed by Queer Theatre Kalamazoo, Milky Way Theatre (Off-Broadway), Musical Theatre Factory, Signature Theatre, with performances in Colorado, Georgia, Michigan, Pennsylvania, and Portugal. His artistic talents led to his Working Title Playwrights apprenticeship, Playwrights Foundation fellowship, Great Performances Fellowship Award, and 78th Tony Awards CBS appearance. They hold a B.S. in Astrophysics with honors and Minor in Theatre from Michigan State University.
